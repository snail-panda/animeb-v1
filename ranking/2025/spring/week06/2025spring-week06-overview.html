Spring 2025 – Week 6 Overview

May 18, 2025

Introduction

We've now reached Week 6 of the Spring 2025 anime season, marking the halfway point of the cour.
Interestingly, this midpoint arrived with a rather subdued tone.
Instead of dramatic shakeups or bold shifts, many shows took a quieter, more gradual approach—steadily building upon what they’ve established so far, or subtly transitioning without fanfare.
It was a week defined by calm progression rather than explosive change.

So, how did things actually unfold? Let’s take a closer look together.

Highlights and Thoughts

Highlights & Thoughts – Week 6

As mentioned earlier, Week 6 marked a relatively calm midpoint for the Spring 2025 anime season. But that doesn’t mean it was dull—far from it. Several shows delivered truly remarkable episodes this week.

Let’s dive right in.

1st Place – Sword of the Demon Hunter (Kijin Gentoushou)

Once again, this series continues to impress with its masterfully layered storytelling. This week focused on the theme of parent-child relationships—particularly between Jinya and his biological father, as well as his adoptive father who took him and his sister in after they left home.

The episode cleverly uses an ukiyo-e print, tainted by scandalous rumors, as the lens through which these complex family bonds are explored. The result is a touching, profound portrayal of both blood ties and found family. Whether it’s the weight of shared blood or the strength of bonds forged without it, the story beautifully illustrates the nuances of familial love.

Especially powerful was the silent exchange of sake between Jinya and his estranged father. Though neither openly acknowledged their relationship, the unspoken understanding was undeniable—subtle, emotional, and unforgettable. And just when we felt that warmth, a chilling sense of foreboding crept in. To top it off, the storyline involving Kihei, the soba shop owner, and his adopted daughter Ofuro added yet another rich layer. Absolutely phenomenal.

2nd Place – Shoshimin: How to Become Ordinary 2 (Shoushimin Series 2nd Season)

This week wrapped up the "Kuri-kinton Incident" with resounding brilliance. For a deeper breakdown, please refer to the detailed review section, but let me touch on the highlights.

The episode explored the dynamic between Osanai, Kobato, and Uri through an extended dessert metaphor: kuri-kinton (sweet chestnut paste) and marron glacé. The former is bound with water and molded into a unified whole, while the latter is delicately coated in sugar, its sweetness developed layer by layer. Yet a single misstep in the process can ruin everything—a perfect allegory for Uri’s critical mistake.

This parallel was not only poetic but emotionally devastating. Osanai’s terrifying expression in the aftermath said more than words ever could. Why didn’t I rank this first? Why didn’t I give it 11 points? I have no idea. It was stunning, simply stunning. A brilliant, unforgettable episode.

3rd Place – Me and the Alien MuMu (Uchuujin MuuMuu)

This show continues to be an unexpected delight. The episode revolved around a rice cooker, the heroine, MuuMuu, the handsome lead, and his adorably ugly cat. Despite the seemingly slow progress in the overarching narrative, the evolving dynamics between the heroine and the male lead—and between the lead and his cat—were explored in fresh and humorous ways.

It was a fun, well-executed episode that brought these relationships closer together through small but meaningful developments.

4th Place – Witch Watch (Witch Watch)

Still under probation for now, but this week was surprisingly solid. The balance of comedy and seriousness was handled well, and the episode wasn’t overloaded with parodies or homages. When it focuses on telling a proper story, the show proves it can really deliver. It was genuinely enjoyable.

We’ll continue monitoring it to see if it earns a full return to the ranked list. For now, it holds a tentative spot.

5th Place – Apocalypse Hotel (Apocalypse Hotel)

Another standout episode, though reactions will likely vary depending on the viewer. It leaned heavily into an American-style narrative structure and visuals—not just due to its Avengers-like parody elements but in its overall framing.

I personally loved it. The tension between Hachio and the mysterious visitor who claims to be here to destroy fate was gripping. The ambiguous investigator character also added intrigue. I enjoy these kinds of interpretative dynamics.

This week, The Apothecary Diaries took a break, but the other shows stepped up admirably. A fulfilling and enjoyable week overall.

Additional Commentary – Not Ranked

6th Place – Your Forma (Your Forma)

This is a frustrating one. The story itself isn’t bad, but the execution is weak. The animation is poor, the heroine lacks appeal and consistency in design, and the plot relies heavily on convenient coincidences. What were the cat and boss even for?

It’s a shame. There’s potential here, but the show doesn’t seem to know what to do with it.

7th Place – From Old Country Bumpkin to Master Swordsman (Katainaka no Ossan, Kensei ni Naru)

This one may work for some viewers, but it really didn’t click for me. The main character is endlessly humble and self-deprecating to a point that becomes grating. It reminds me a bit of Tanjiro from Demon Slayer narrating and explaining everything nonstop.

While the relationship with the girl he begins living with, Miu-chan, had some charm, the development was too sudden and predictable. The whole thing felt too staged and hard to get emotionally invested in.

Last Place – Summer Pockets (Summer Pockets)

This week’s episode was a total mess. Suddenly, everything was thrown onto the deck: everyone shows up, they travel to an isolated island, meet the mother, hear the full backstory—is she even real? A ghost? It was utter chaos.

It seems like the show is trying to cram in each heroine’s route in six episodes, but the execution is sloppy beyond belief. I honestly don’t know why I’m still watching or scoring this. Just awful.

Conclusion

That concludes the Week 5 edition of the Weekly Anime Rankings.

This week again, the Top 5—no, Top 6—were all so outstanding that any of them could have taken the #1 spot. This spring season is truly filled with masterpieces. Or rather, it’s not just that the shows are great—it’s that each of them delivers something genuinely amazing every single week. It’s getting to the point where I can’t wait for the next episode.
At the same time, they’re all so incredible that each episode feels emotionally heavy, in a good way. It makes me want to take my time with each show rather than rush through a long list. I want to savor them, one by one.

As for the rest of the lineup, I hope to see a bit more effort. As mentioned earlier, we dropped four titles this week. In addition to those, both From Old Country Bumpkin to Master Swordsman (片田舎のおっさん、剣聖になる / Katainaka no Ossan, Kensei ni Naru) and Can a Boy-Girl Friendship Survive? (男女の友情は成立する？（いや、しないっ!!） / Danjo no Yuujou wa Seiritsu suru? (Iya, Shinai!!)) are starting to look pretty rough. I hope they can bounce back.

All in all, it was another absolutely fantastic week.
Next week marks the halfway point—Week 6—and things are only going to heat up from here.
I’ll see you all again next week!
