Top 5 (Ranked Works)

  • 1st – Turkey! Time to Strike (Turkey!)

    See the full impressions in the review; here’s just a quick note.

    Once again, the show’s manic energy, sheer commitment, and that wild, over-the-top final stretch completely bowled me over.

    Yes, on paper it’s “you know the type,” and sure, it looks just as absurd as the glut of isekai-reincarnation shows out there—especially, recent ones. But unlike those hollow messes, this one's driven by real passion, with a throughline you can actually feel. And not to mention, it's not isekai or reincarnation at all—it's just plain time travel.


  • 2nd – Dr. Stone: Science Future Part 2 (Dr. STONE SCIENCE FUTURE – Final Season, 2nd Cour)

    Full thoughts are in the review; here’s the quick take.

    Now that we’re deep into the endgame, the show has been relentlessly intense—shaking me to the core again and again. Everything the cast has worked toward has crystallized, and you can feel that unshakable conviction in every moment. Even Xeno and Stanley, with all their history, act without hesitation or doubt. As Senku himself put it: they don’t “choose” their path—they simply do what must be done. That resolve hit hard, regardless of which side they’re on.

    Honestly, next week feels like it’ll be an inevitable tearjerker. Please, anything but the cruelest version of a revival—like one poor survivor finally crafting revival fluid, only to… yeah, I couldn’t bear it.


  • 3rd – April Showers Bring May Flowers (Busu ni Hanataba wo.)

    As with the others, see the review for the full impressions. Here’s the short version.

    Uguisudani finally had her big moment—but it worked precisely because of everything she’s gone through up until now. The portrayal wasn’t flashy, but it was packed with sincerity and warmth, and it landed beautifully.

    Tabata’s grounded humanity continues to shine, and Gotanda was burning with passion this week too. The ending—Shimbashi, Ueno, and the band’s performance—didn’t try to play it up theatrically. Instead, it projected their feelings plainly, as they are. That honesty made it hit even harder. Shimbashi, respect. And Ueno too.

    I really like this cast. They’re all solid, likable, and believable.


  • 4th – The Fragrant Flower Blooms with Dignity (Kaoru Hana wa Rin to Saku)

    Details are in the review—here’s the quick impression.

    Unfair again! And this time it’s the father. (I’d honestly thought this was a single-mother household—just me?)

    Once again, the show kept things grounded instead of melodramatic, which made the story feel genuine. This week’s episode, like Rintarou’s cake, was warm, sweet, and tinged with a few tears.

    Subaru was great again—but is she maybe a little too good to be true? (Not a complaint—she’s awesome.)


  • 5th – Me and the Alien MuMu (Uchuujin MuMu)

    See the review for the full breakdown; here’s just the short note.

    Once again, right before the finale, the show picked up speed. It wasn’t about one standout moment so much as the overall tempo and direction—it was sharp, fun, and engaging from start to finish.

    Short note this time, but that’s how it felt. Personally, I’d love to see a sequel—here’s hoping.



Those were the Top 5 quick takes. Now let’s briefly touch on a few notable works outside the main ranking.


Spotlight

Solo Camping for Two (Futari Solo Camp)

Again, see the review for the full writeup—this is just the short take.

It was one of those episodes where every little part clicked. There’s no one huge standout, but the whole package landed smoothly, with a nice balance of fun and comfort. Honestly, it reminded me of why I picked up this show in the first place—it’s just a straightforward, satisfying watch.


Editor’s Pick

Clevatess (Clevatess: Majuu no Ou to Akago to Kabane no Yuusha)

Full thoughts are in the review; here’s the short version.

Once again, this show proves it knows how to handle its own darkness. The story’s still brutal, but it doesn’t drown in edge for edge’s sake—the beats carry weight, and you can feel the intent behind them. It’s not pretty, but it’s compelling. That’s why it earned this week’s Editor’s Pick spot.


Dookie

Rascal Does Not Dream of Santa Claus (Seishun Buta Yarou wa Santa Claus no Yume wo Minai)

Details are in the review—this is the short note.

I just can’t get into it. The characters feel hollow, and the emotional beats don’t land. There are a few sparks—like Mai showing her star power—but they can’t carry the weight of the cour. For me, it’s more frustrating than enjoyable, and that lands it in the Dookie slot this week.
