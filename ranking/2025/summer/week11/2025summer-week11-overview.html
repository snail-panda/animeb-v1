Summer 2025 – Week 11 Overview

September 20, 2025

Introduction

We’ve now reached Week 11 of the Summer 2025 anime season.

With most shows landing on Episodes 11 or 12—and within our ranking pool, no fewer than six titles wrapped up their runs this week. The rest are right in the thick of their climaxes, so things must be heating up finally (I wish…).

So then—how did this week’s ranking turn out? Let’s dive in and take a look!

Top 5 (Ranked Works)

  • 1st – Dr. Stone: Science Future Part 2 (Dr.STONE SCIENCE FUTURE – Final Season, 2nd Cour)

    See the review section for full thoughts. Below is just a brief impression.

    All the praise in the world goes to Suika. Watching this episode without tears? Impossible. What kept her going for seven long years was the memory of her friends—and more than that, the look in their eyes even after petrification, eyes that never lost their light. Senku’s final words of praise brought everything home, and yes, the tears too.

    Honestly, like Senku said, I’m just glad it was “only seven years.” If it had been decades, I don’t think I could’ve recovered.


  • 2nd – April Showers Bring May Flowers (Busu ni Hanataba wo.)

    Full review is available as always—this is the short version.

    Gotanda really showed up this week—absolute respect. It’s the kind of moment that makes you think, “That’s the kind of guy I want to be.” And thanks to that, Uguisudani’s growth hit even harder. These two just keep getting better.

    Now Ueno has confessed, and with just one episode left, I can’t wait to see how it all ends.


  • 3rd – Rascal Does Not Dream of Santa Claus (Seishun Buta Yarou wa Santa Claus no Yume wo Minai)

    As always, the full review is up—here’s a quick summary.

    After all this time, the Kirishima Touko subplot is finally going somewhere. Things are getting more interesting again. Honestly, the story doesn’t really work unless we reach this final stretch—it’s all about the payoff.

    Also, we didn’t get any Mai/Sakuta old-couple banter this week, which (personally) was kind of a relief.


  • 4th – Me and the Alien MuMu (Uchuujin MuMu)

    See the review section for full impressions—here’s a quick one.

    The show has now reached its finale. But this week’s ranking isn’t just about the final episode—it’s recognition for two full cours of fun, well-paced storytelling that wrapped things up nicely. I really wish it could keep going. Hoping for a Season 2!

    Sakurako, Samezu, the ikemen—what happens to this triangle? I want to know!


  • 5th – Ruri Rocks (Ruri no Houseki)

    As with the others, check the review for full thoughts—this is just the short take.

    Another tightly written episode. What started as a simple interest in pretty stones has evolved—now it’s about how Ruri sees her future, shaped by everything she’s experienced along the way. The show keeps finding ways to deepen its core themes.

    Meeting Nagi, Imari, and Garasu—and what she’s learned from them—has helped Ruri move forward. That’s why she was able to hear the “voice of the Seiryuu Legend” and discover the cluster of sapphires at the end of this journey.



That wraps up our quick takes on the Top 5. Now let’s move on to a few spotlight titles that didn’t make the ranking but still caught our attention—some for good reasons, others… not so much.


Spotlight

My Dress-Up Darling Season 2 (Sono Bisque Doll wa Koi wo Suru Season 2)

Once again, the production quality was top-notch—truly impressive. And while I didn’t dislike it (Asahi’s reactions were a highlight), I couldn’t help but feel like the final episode left things strangely unresolved, both in tone and structure. It made me wonder: what was this cour ultimately trying to say?

*See the full review in the ranking table. (Heads-up: it might lean a little negative.)


Editor’s Pick

The Fragrant Flower Blooms with Dignity (Kaoru Hana wa Rin to Saku)

Watching this episode, I felt embarrassed for forgetting that our local Shichirigahama beach is actually a no-swimming zone. For a second, I found myself thinking, “Why aren’t they in swimsuits?” or “High schoolers not swimming in the summer? Come on!”

Despite some mixed feelings—nothing huge, just a lingering lack of clarity—the episode was overall strong and heartfelt, earning it a spot here. Looking ahead to the finale, I’m curious how it’ll wrap up. Among the CloverWorks trio this season, this one has definitely been my favorite.

*More thoughts in the review, if you’re curious.


Dookie

Rent-a-Girlfriend Season 4 (Kanojo, Okarishimasu 4th Season)

An absolutely devastating finale—in all the worst ways. If you need a number to sum it up: this one scored a 3. That’s all you need to know. The only saving grace? Grandma and the parents. Otherwise, this was just a drawn-out “confession tease” mess. Still, you’ve got to give it credit for being spectacularly bad.

*See the review table for the full lament.
